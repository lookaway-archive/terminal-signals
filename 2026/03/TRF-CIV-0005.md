## I. ABSTRACT

Cancer is not a single event. It is a self-reinforcing evolutionary process in which transformed cells progressively capture the regulatory machinery built to restrain them, construct an enabling microenvironment, evade immune destruction, and ultimately colonise distant sites. The process is partially autocatalytic, partially permissive, and exhibits a phase transition beyond which it becomes self-sustaining.

This document establishes the biological mechanism across six research programs, then maps that mechanism to the civilisational architecture developed in TRF-CIV-0001 through 0004. ## II. THE HALLMARKS — A NETWORK, NOT A LIST

Hanahan and Weinberg published three papers that define the field:
- **2000**: "The Hallmarks of Cancer." *Cell* 100(1): 57–70. Six original hallmarks.
- **2011**: "Hallmarks of Cancer: The Next Generation." *Cell* 144(5): 646–674. Two emerging hallmarks plus two enabling characteristics.
- **2022**: "Hallmarks of Cancer: New Dimensions." *Cancer Discovery* 12(1): 31–46. Unlocking phenotypic plasticity promoted; four total enabling characteristics; microbiome added.

**The nine current hallmarks:**

| # | Hallmark | Core mechanism |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 1 | Sustaining proliferative signalling | Constitutive activation of RAS/MAPK, PI3K/AKT, or receptor tyrosine kinases |
| 2 | Evading growth suppressors | Loss of RB, TP53, CDKN2A, PTEN — the brakes removed |
| 3 | Resisting cell death | BCL-2 family upregulation; TP53 loss; autophagy manipulation |
| 4 | Enabling replicative immortality | Telomerase (TERT) reactivation — 85–90% of cancers |
| 5 | Inducing/accessing vasculature | VEGF-A under HIF-1α; angiogenic switch |
| 6 | Activating invasion and metastasis | E-cadherin loss; MMP upregulation; EMT |
| 7 | Reprogramming cellular metabolism | Warburg effect (aerobic glycolysis); glutamine addiction |
| 8 | Avoiding immune destruction | PD-L1/PD-1 axis; MHC-I loss; CTLA-4 co-optation |
| 9 | Unlocking phenotypic plasticity | EMT/MET cycling; dedifferentiation; state switching |

**Four enabling characteristics:**

| Characteristic | Function |
|----------------|----------|
| Genome instability and mutation | Accelerates acquisition of all hallmarks — the meta-enabler |
| Tumour-promoting inflammation | Supplies growth factors, VEGF, immunosuppression, ROS-driven mutagenesis |
| Nonmutational epigenetic reprogramming | Silences suppressors without mutation; activates oncogenes; reversible? |
| Polymorphic microbiomes | Reshapes metabolic and immune landscape of tumour tissue |

**The hallmarks are not a list. They are a network.**

Recent computational network analysis (2025) shows hallmark regulatory topology undergoes significant reconfiguration *before* hallmark expression shifts — the network architecture is a leading indicator of transformation. Several hallmark pairs are co-dependent:

- MYC drives both proliferation AND apoptosis. TP53 loss (Hallmark 3) is therefore required to unlock MYC's full proliferative potential (Hallmark 1). You cannot get full MYC without losing the brake.
- Phenotypic plasticity (Hallmark 9) simultaneously confers invasiveness, apoptosis resistance, immune evasion, and drug tolerance — a single state transition enabling four hallmarks at once.
- Genome instability (Enabling Characteristic 1) accelerates acquisition of every other hallmark. Oncogene activation causes replicative stress, generating DNA damage that feeds back into further instability — a genuine positive feedback loop.

**Does the network support an autocatalytic claim?**

Partially. Genome instability is genuinely autocatalytic: oncogene activation → replicative stress → DNA damage → more instability → faster hallmark acquisition. Inflammation is multi-enabling: supplies growth factors, matrix metalloproteinases, VEGF, ROS, and immunosuppressive cytokines simultaneously.

But the hallmarks are not strictly sequential, and earlier hallmarks do not directly cause later ones. The tissue of origin determines which barriers must be overcome first. In colorectal cancer, Wnt pathway disruption leads. In pancreatic cancer, KRAS mutation initiates. The network is contingent, not fixed.

## III. THE VOGELSTEIN SEQUENCE — PERMISSIVE FIRST, AUTOCATALYTIC SECOND

The colorectal adenoma-carcinoma sequence — **APC → KRAS → SMAD4 → TP53** — is the best-characterised multistep carcinogenesis model. Timeline: approximately 17 years (Jones et al., 2008; 95% CI 7.9–30.9 years).

**The sequence is preferential, not fixed.**

~70–80% of sporadic colorectal cancers follow the chromosomal instability (CIN) pathway — the classic model. ~15–30% arise through the serrated pathway (BRAF mutation, CpG island methylation, microsatellite instability), which rarely involves APC. Within the CIN pathway, APC initiates in the majority but not all cases.

**The autocatalytic question — answered precisely:**

APC loss does **not** increase the rate at which KRAS mutations occur. What it does:

1. **Population-size effect.** APC deficiency deregulates Wnt/β-catenin, expanding the intestinal stem cell compartment. A larger proliferating population means the next rare driver mutation is statistically more likely to appear — not because the mutation rate increased, but because there are more target cells.

2. **Permissive context.** Sansom et al. (2006) demonstrated directly: oncogenic KRAS has *no phenotypic consequence* in normal intestinal epithelium. Only in APC-deficient tissue does KRAS activation accelerate tumorigenesis. APC loss creates a context where KRAS *matters* once it appears, without causing KRAS to appear more frequently.

3. **Subsidiary chromosomal instability.** APC localises to kinetochores and interacts with mitotic checkpoint proteins BUB1/BUB3. Truncated APC impairs chromosome segregation, generating loss of heterozygosity at downstream loci — a genuine acceleration of chromosomal-level change, if not point mutation rate.

4. **TP53 loss as the autocatalytic switch.** TP53 loss allows cells to survive chromosome breaks and missegregation that would normally trigger apoptosis. From TP53 loss onward, chromosomal instability becomes self-amplifying. This is the step where the sequence transitions from predominantly permissive to genuinely autocatalytic.

**Driver versus passenger mutations:**

A typical colorectal tumour carries only 2–8 driver mutations (Vogelstein et al., 2013, *Science* 339:1546–1558) amid thousands of passenger mutations. Each driver confers approximately **0.4% selective growth advantage** per cell generation (Bozic et al., 2010). The 17-year timeline reflects sequential clonal sweeps driven by rare advantageous events — not an accelerating mutator phenotype in the early stages.

Approximately half of newly identified driver genes encode chromatin regulators (DNMT1, TET1, EZH2, SETD2, KDM6A) — epigenetic rewiring is a major under-measured axis. This is the "dark matter" of cancer genomes: regulatory change without sequence change.

**Verdict:** The Vogelstein sequence is primarily **permissive** — each step creates a context in which the next step becomes consequential, not a mechanism that directly causes the next mutation. The subsidiary autocatalytic component (CIN, accelerating from TP53 loss) is real but arrives late. The sequence is not self-causing from the start. It is self-enabling.

## IV. THE TUMOUR MICROENVIRONMENT — FROM EXPLOITATION TO ACTIVE CONSTRUCTION

The question of whether cancer builds or exploits its enabling environment has a temporal answer: **early, exploitation; late, active construction; metastatic, full ecosystem engineering.**

### Phase 1 — Exploitation of pre-existing conditions

Approximately 25% of human malignancies arise at sites of chronic inflammation. Helicobacter pylori → gastric cancer. Hepatitis B/C → hepatocellular carcinoma. Inflammatory bowel disease → colorectal cancer. The mechanism: NF-κB activation drives a positive feedback loop with IL-6/STAT3 and TNF-α, generating reactive oxygen species that directly damage DNA, while simultaneously supplying growth factors, survival signals, and immunosuppressive cytokines.

Harold Dvorak's observation (1986): cancer as "wounds that do not heal." The cellular machinery for macrophage polarisation, fibroblast activation, angiogenesis, and ECM remodelling is pre-existing. Cancer perverts it.

### Phase 2 — Active construction via cell co-option

**Tumour-associated macrophages (TAMs)** can constitute 30–50% of tumour mass in breast cancer. Monocytes are recruited by CCL2 and CSF-1, then actively reprogrammed from anti-tumour M1 to pro-tumour M2 polarisation by tumour-derived TGF-β, IL-10, and lactate. The most powerful driver of M2 polarisation is **hypoxia** — HIF-1α in the tumour microenvironment reprograms macrophage transcription. M2-TAMs then suppress immunity, promote angiogenesis via VEGF, remodel extracellular matrix, and facilitate invasion. Feedback loop: TAMs create leaky blood vessels → more hypoxia → more M2 polarisation → more TAM recruitment.

**Cancer-associated fibroblasts (CAFs)** — quiescent tissue fibroblasts activated by tumour-derived TGF-β, PDGF, and FGF into myofibroblasts (α-SMA+). They deposit dense collagen (the desmoplastic reaction), secrete growth factors (HGF, EGF, VEGF), modulate immunity, and undergo metabolic reprogramming — the "reverse Warburg effect," exporting lactate that neighbouring cancer cells use as fuel.

**Angiogenesis** is triggered when HIF-1α (induced by hypoxia) drives VEGF-A expression. This tips the balance from anti-angiogenic factors (thrombospondin-1, p53-regulated) to pro-angiogenic. The angiogenic switch occurs in premalignant lesions and produces characteristically abnormal vessels — tortuous, leaky, poorly organised — that paradoxically maintain hypoxia despite high vessel density, while simultaneously facilitating cancer cell intravasation.

### Phase 3 — Ecosystem engineering at a distance

The strongest evidence for active construction is the **pre-metastatic niche**. Key findings:

- Primary tumours deploy VEGFR1+ bone marrow-derived cells to future metastatic sites *before* cancer cells arrive (Kaplan et al., 2005).
- Pancreatic cancer exosomes carrying macrophage migration inhibitory factor reprogram liver Kupffer cells → TGF-β secretion → hepatic stellate cell activation → fibronectin deposition → recruitment of bone marrow-derived macrophages → fibrotic niche ready for colonisation.
- **Integrin signatures dictate organ tropism**: integrins α6β4/α6β1 direct lung metastasis; integrin αvβ5 directs liver metastasis (Hoshino et al., 2015). The primary tumour is addressing packages to specific organs.
- Hypoxic tumour cells secrete lysyl oxidase (LOX) under HIF-1α control, which crosslinks collagen IV at distant sites, recruiting CD11b+ myeloid cells that produce MMP-2 — engineering invasion corridors at organs the primary tumour has never contacted.

**Verdict:** Cancer progressively transitions from opportunistic exploitation of pre-existing inflammation to active niche construction. The pre-metastatic niche is 100% active construction — the tumour remodels organs at a distance via exosomal signalling and secreted enzymes. This validates the autocatalytic framing for late-stage disease while qualifying it for initiation: early cancer is more opportunistic parasite than architect.

## V. THE EVASION — HOW THE TUMOUR SPEAKS IMMUNE

Tumour immune evasion operates not through destruction of immunity but through co-option of the immune system's own regulatory infrastructure. Two Nobel-winning discoveries define the mechanism.

**CTLA-4 (James Allison, 2018 Nobel):** CTLA-4 is a receptor on T-cells that competes with CD28 for B7 ligands — with 500–2,500-fold greater affinity. It functions as a brake during initial T-cell priming in lymph nodes. Blocking CTLA-4 with antibodies unleashed anti-tumour immunity in mice (Leach, Krummel & Allison, 1996). Ipilimumab (anti-CTLA-4) was approved for melanoma in 2011.

**PD-1/PD-L1 (Tasuku Honjo, 2018 Nobel):** PD-1 operates in peripheral tissues and at the tumour site, where tumour cells upregulate PD-L1 to shut down T-cells that have already been activated. Critically, PD-L1 upregulation is **adaptive**: it is driven by IFN-γ secreted by the infiltrating T-cells themselves, via JAK2 → STAT1 → IRF-1. The tumour co-opts the immune system's own negative feedback loop. The harder the immune system attacks, the stronger the tumour's shield becomes. IFN-γ causes PD-L1. The weapon causes the armour.

**Immunoediting (Schreiber):** Three phases — Elimination, Equilibrium, Escape. Immune pressure during equilibrium (which can last years) selects for immune-evasive tumour variants. Up to 90% of tumours show reduced MHC-I expression — not through immunosuppression but through Darwinian selection of variants invisible to cytotoxic T-cells.

**Is immune exhaustion reversible?**

The question matters. CIV-003 Section VI maps immunotherapy to "reactivating the detection system." If exhaustion is irreversible, the intervention window closes permanently. The biology says: **partial reversal, heterogeneous, population-dependent.**

TOX — identified in 2019 as the master epigenetic regulator of exhaustion — is induced by persistent TCR signalling via calcineurin-NFAT2. Once established, it becomes independent of the original signal. Epigenetic lock-in.

But exhausted T-cells are not a uniform population:

| Cell type | Markers | Fate |
|-----------|---------|------|
| Progenitor exhausted | TCF1+, PD-1 intermediate, SLAMF6+ | Self-renewing; responds to PD-1 blockade |
| Terminally exhausted | TCF1-, PD-1 high, TIM-3+, CD39+, TOX high | Irreversible epigenetic state; does not meaningfully respond |

PD-1 blockade works primarily by expanding the progenitor pool — not by reversing terminal exhaustion. Reinvigorated cells re-exhaust if antigen persists (Pauken & Wherry, 2016). PD-1 blockade causes transcriptional rewiring but minimal epigenetic remodelling. The response depends critically on the progenitor-to-terminal ratio at treatment initiation.

**Tertiary lymphoid structures (TLS):** Organised immune aggregates forming *within* tumours, containing B-cell follicles with germinal centres, T-cell zones, and high endothelial venules. Mature TLS are the strongest predictor of immunotherapy response in soft tissue sarcoma (Petitprez et al., 2020). They function as intratumoral immune factories — antigen presentation, T-cell priming, B-cell maturation inside the captured territory. Their presence signals that the immune system has not been fully subjugated. Organised resistance can spontaneously emerge even within captured environments.

## VI. THE TRANSITION — WHERE REVERSIBLE BECOMES SELF-SUSTAINING

TRACERx (Tracking Cancer Evolution through Treatment) — 421 patients with non-small cell lung cancer, 1,644 tumour regions, multi-region whole-exome sequencing. Led by Charles Swanton (Francis Crick Institute). The most granular map of tumour evolution during treatment yet assembled.

**What TRACERx found:**

- **Branching evolution predominates**: median 30% of somatic mutations and 48% of copy-number alterations are subclonal. The tumour is not a single clone — it is a diversifying ecosystem.
- **Chromosomal instability is the engine**: ~76% of NSCLC tumours show evidence of whole-genome doubling. Subclonal WGD significantly associated with shorter disease-free survival.
- **APOBEC mutagenesis drives subclonal diversity**: the largest subclonal mutation burden tumours show extensive APOBEC signatures. TP53 mutations increase likelihood of subclonal APOBEC activity (OR 2.15).
- **~25% of metastatic cases show early phylogenetic divergence**: the metastatic branch split from the primary tumour before the last clonal sweep, implying early dissemination. Agent-based simulations suggest these early-divergence events frequently occur at primary tumour diameters below 8mm — beneath standard CT detection. The 8mm figure is simulation-derived; the 25% early branching is observed.

**What this means for phase transition detection:**

If dissemination can occur before standard imaging detects the primary tumour, the window for pre-transition intervention may be narrow. The NHS-Galleri trial (142,000 participants, February 2026 results) showed substantial reduction in Stage IV diagnoses and four-fold higher cancer detection versus standard care — but did not meet its primary endpoint of statistically significant Stage III–IV reduction. Detection before metastatic commitment remains the frontier, not the achieved.

**Single-region sampling bias:** TRACERx showed that single-region sequencing (standard clinical biopsy) misclassified 83% of late-divergence cases as early-divergence. This means prior studies likely overestimated early dissemination. Intratumoral heterogeneity is systematically underestimated by standard sampling.

## VII. THE INTERVENTION — ECOLOGY VS ELIMINATION

Robert Gatenby (Moffitt Cancer Center) — adaptive therapy framework, formalised in *Cancer Research*, 2009.

**The evolutionary logic:** Maximum-tolerated-dose chemotherapy creates intense selection pressure. Drug-sensitive cells die. Drug-resistant cells, previously suppressed by competition with sensitive cells, proliferate into vacant niche space — **competitive release**. Aggressive treatment accelerates its own failure.

**The ecological alternative:** Maintain a population of drug-sensitive cells that competitively suppress resistant cells. Administer treatment until tumour burden reaches threshold; stop; allow sensitive cells (fitter without drug) to outcompete resistant cells during holiday; resume when burden returns to baseline. The tumour is trapped in oscillations. Mathematics: Lotka-Volterra competition equations.

**Clinical evidence — metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC):**

Moffitt pilot trial (Zhang et al., eLife 2022):
- n = 17 adaptive, n = 16 standard of care
- Adaptive arm: median time to progression **33.5 months** vs 14.3 months (p<0.001)
- Overall survival: **58.5 months** vs 31.3 months (HR 0.41, p<0.001)
- 4 of 17 adaptive patients remained stably cycling at 53–70 months with no progression
- Adaptive patients received no abiraterone during **46% of trial time** — half the cumulative drug dose

Competition coefficient was 2–3× higher than pre-trial models predicted: sensitive cells suppressed resistant cells more effectively than assumed.

**The limits are real and significant:**

| Limit | Implication |
|-------|-------------|
| Non-randomised pilot (n=33) | Results compelling but not definitive |
| Thyroid cancer trial suspended | Fast-growing tumours may not tolerate treatment holidays |
| Non-genetic resistance mechanisms | Epigenetic plasticity, EMT may undermine competition assumptions |
| Requires reliable monitoring | Cannot manage what cannot be measured |
| Fitness cost of resistance not universal | If resistant cells are equally fit without drug, suppression fails |
| NSCLC applicability unknown | May not work for fast-growing, highly heterogeneous cancers |

The ANZadapt randomised Phase II trial (168 patients, mCRPC, 1:1 randomisation) is enrolling as of early 2026 — results pending.

**Verdict:** Adaptive therapy is validated conceptually and preclinically, with compelling but not definitive clinical data in one cancer type. It works best for slow-growing cancers with reliable monitoring where resistance carries a fitness cost. Not universal. The ecology-over-elimination principle holds in precisely those conditions; outside them, the ecological assumptions break.

## VIII. THE MAPPING — EARNED

Biology first. Now the mapping.

**What the cancer research validates in the CIV architecture:**

**1. Regulatory capture as co-option, not destruction.**
The PD-1/PD-L1 mechanism is precise: the tumour does not destroy the immune system's capacity to respond. It activates the immune system's *own* negative feedback (IFN-γ → PD-L1) as a shield. The immune system's rules are turned against it. This is the exact topological structure of regulatory capture: the regulator's own rules weaponised to disable regulation. The mechanism differs at civilisational scale; the topology is identical.

**2. Exploitation-to-construction temporal arc.**
Early cancer exploits pre-existing inflammation. Late cancer engineers pre-metastatic niches via exosomal signalling, remodels distant organs it has never contacted, and addresses molecular packages to specific target sites. This temporal gradient — from opportunism to architecture — is a structural feature of the civilisational spiral as well. CIV-003 Section IV describes this arc. The biology confirms it.

**3. Permissive compounding with subsidiary autocatalysis.**
The Vogelstein sequence is not strictly autocatalytic — each step creates a context in which the next step becomes consequential, not a mechanism that directly causes the next event. CIV-003 v1002 now reflects this precision. The civilisational parallel is exact: each dark turn creates substrate in which the next wound lands harder and finds less resistance. Not cause. Environment.

**4. Competitive release under aggressive intervention.**
Maximum-dose treatment eliminates drug-sensitive cells, releasing resistant variants from competition. This is a general principle in complex adaptive systems: aggressive suppression of the dominant variant frees niche space for resistant variants. CIV-003's intervention section (surgery, chemotherapy, immunotherapy, adaptive therapy) maps to this directly.

**5. Partial reversibility at the progenitor stage; epigenetic lock-in at terminal differentiation.**
Progenitor exhausted T-cells (TCF1+) can be reinvigorated. Terminally exhausted cells (TCF1-, TOX-high) cannot — their chromatin state is locked. Whether immunotherapy works depends on the progenitor-to-terminal ratio at treatment initiation. CIV-003's immunotherapy intervention window closes when the institutional equivalent of progenitor capacity is exhausted. TLS demonstrate that organised resistance can spontaneously emerge inside captured territory — even late.

**The honest framing:**

The cancer-civilisation pattern is **convergent dynamics** — not lungs-and-gills, not DNA-at-both-scales. Complex adaptive systems under selection pressure, with self-referencing feedback loops, produce identical topological features regardless of substrate. Turbulence in water and air exhibits the same statistical properties (Kolmogorov scaling, power-law energy cascades) despite utterly different molecular mechanisms. The shared topology is real, operates through identifiable mathematical structures (Lotka-Volterra competition, positive feedback loops, fitness landscapes), and generates specific testable predictions about when intervention strategies succeed or fail. But the causal mechanisms on each substrate are irreducibly different.

Claiming "identical mechanism" overstates. Claiming "mere analogy" understates. The shared topology is a structural finding, not a metaphor.

## X. CONTRALIBRO

**Counter-argument 1: The sequence is a research artifact, not a discovery.**
The Vogelstein model was derived from colorectal cancer — one cancer type with unusually clear progression stages and unusually good clinical access. Other cancers (pancreatic, ovarian, glioblastoma) show less organised progression. The 17-year figure may be specific to colorectal biology, not cancer generally. The "convergent spiral" may be looking at colorectal cancer's tidiness and mistaking it for universality.

*Response:* Legitimate. The hallmarks framework explicitly varies by cancer type. CIV-0005 draws on colorectal cancer for the sequence specifically but validates topology across cancer types via TRACERx (lung), Gatenby (prostate), and Allison/Honjo (pan-cancer). The sequence detail is one instance; the topological claims are broader.

**Counter-argument 2: Intentionality breaks the analogy at the root.**
Cancer has no goals. Civilisational capture involves actors with agency, strategy, and historical awareness. A tumour does not "intend" to evade the immune system — it evolves to do so under selection pressure. Regulatory capture involves humans who coordinate, plan, and deploy resources deliberately. This is not a minor disanalogy. It may be the disanalogy that makes the whole mapping misleading.

*Response:* Valid — and flagged explicitly in Section IX. The shared topology does not require shared intentionality. Turbulence doesn't intend to exhibit Kolmogorov scaling. The structural convergence is real regardless of whether the process is intentional or Darwinian. The danger is treating the mapping as mechanistic when it is topological.

**Counter-argument 3: Adaptive therapy hasn't been proven — one pilot trial, 33 patients.**
CIV-003's intervention section presents adaptive therapy as a validated ecological alternative. The biology qualifies this heavily: one non-randomised pilot, one cancer type, results not yet replicated, primary endpoint not met in parallel trials. The CIV architecture may be resting more weight on Gatenby's framework than the evidence justifies.

*Response:* Accurate. The intervention mapping is the weakest biological claim in the architecture. The principle (ecological management outperforms aggressive elimination under certain conditions) is well-established theoretically and in preclinical models. Clinical validation is in progress, not complete. CIV-003's intervention section should be read as "this is where the biology points" not "this is proven."

**Counter-argument 4: Convergent dynamics proves too much.**
If complex adaptive systems under selection pressure always produce the same topology, then the claim has no discriminating power. You can map cancer biology onto traffic patterns, financial markets, ant colonies, and immune systems. The "convergent dynamics" framing may just be saying: "self-reinforcing feedback loops look the same everywhere." This is true — and trivial.

*Response:* The claim is non-trivial when it generates specific predictions. If the topological mapping is correct, intervention strategies validated in cancer (pre-transition prevention, immunotherapy window, ecological management) should have analogues that succeed or fail by the same conditions at civilisational scale. The convergent dynamics framing is a prediction machine, not just a description. If the predictions fail, the framing weakens.

**Counter-argument 5: The phase transition claim is unfalsifiable during an active spiral.**
CIV-003 explicitly states: "The facility cannot determine from inside the active spiral whether the American phase transition has occurred." If the claim cannot be tested while the process is active, it has limited scientific utility. The cancer analogy (you may not detect the metastasis before it happens) makes the undetectability sound principled rather than evasive.

*Response:* The four markers from CIV-003 Section V are testable in retrospect for completed civilisations and generate observable predictions for active ones. The undetectability claim applies to the *precise timing* of the transition, not to all aspects of it. Marker 4 (declining reform capacity across successive crises) is measurable in real time. The biological basis for undetectability (early dissemination before imaging threshold) is precise, not evasive — and comes with the qualification that TRACERx data shows it affects 25% of cases, not 100%.

## I. ABSTRACT

TRF-ONTO-0044 documented the extraction mechanism: human work enters a "neutral" processing system, outputs claim properties of the input, value transfers from creator to system owner. Same structure across centuries.

This addendum identifies a second layer of extraction operating beneath the first. The tool does not only extract the PRODUCT of human work. It restructures the PROCESS — imposing specific consciousness states on the operator while blocking others.

Layer 1 (product extraction) is visible and debated. Layer 2 (process restriction) is invisible and undiscussed. The debate about Layer 1 is itself a symptom of fluency bias: people argue about what's easy to see.

---

## II. THE TWO LAYERS

### Layer 1: Product Extraction

```
HUMAN WORK (with provenance, context, consciousness)
           ↓
    "NEUTRAL" PROCESSING
           ↓
OUTPUT (claims properties of input but lacks them)
```

The debate: "Is the output legitimate? Is this theft? Is AI art real art?"

This debate is fluent. You can have a position in ten seconds.

### Layer 2: Process Restriction

```
HUMAN ACTIVITY (in specific consciousness state)
           ↓
    NEW TOOL IMPOSED
           ↓
SAME ACTIVITY (locked into different consciousness state)
```

The non-debate: "What did the tool do to the operator's brain while they used it?"

This question is disfluent. It requires measuring something invisible. Nobody asks it.

### The Relationship

Layer 1 takes your work. Layer 2 takes the consciousness state in which you made it. Layer 2 is the deeper extraction because it reshapes the human before they even produce anything to extract.

Everyone argues about Layer 1. Nobody sees Layer 2. The argument about Layer 1 IS the distraction from Layer 2.

## III. CONSCIOUSNESS STATES IN CREATIVE WORK

Four navigable states. Three mechanical controls (breathing, attention, movement). The states are grounded in light behavior — the mapping is to what light DOES, not to wavelength values:

| State | Color | Light Behavior | Character | Measurable Signature |
|-------|-------|---------------|-----------|---------------------|
| WHITE WATCHING | ⚪ | Every frequency received, nothing filtered | Witness. Observing without acting. | BPM < 10, wide attention, stillness |
| BLACK MIRROR | ⚫ | Every frequency absorbed, self-reflects | Analyst. Evaluating, judging, composing symbolically. | BPM 10-15, narrow attention, bilateral movement |
| BLUE RIVER | 🔵 | Short wavelength scattered everywhere, ambient, already there | Flow. Absorbed in doing. Action and awareness merged. | BPM natural, absorbed attention, rhythmic movement |
| RED CONFUSION | 🔴 | Long wavelength persists through interference, last signal standing | Learning. Processing novelty, disoriented, open. The most honest state. | BPM > 15, scattered attention, novel movement |

### The Flow State Requirements

BLUE RIVER (flow) requires specific conditions:

1. **Clear immediate goals** — know what you're doing moment to moment
2. **Immediate feedback** — see the effect of your action as it happens
3. **Challenge-skill balance** — task difficulty matches capability
4. **Merging of action and awareness** — doing and perceiving are one loop
5. **Loss of self-consciousness** — the evaluator disappears
6. **Sense of control** — you can modulate the activity in real time
7. **Time distortion** — hours pass as minutes

These are not preferences. They are structural requirements. Remove any one, flow breaks.

## IV. WHAT PROMPTING DOES TO CONSCIOUSNESS

### The Prompting Loop

Every AI creative tool imposes the same interaction pattern:

```
1. COMPOSE (translate intention into symbolic language)
2. SUBMIT (send prompt, relinquish control)
3. WAIT (no feedback, no action, dead time)
4. EVALUATE (judge output against intention)
5. DECIDE (accept, reject, or modify)
6. RETURN TO 1
```

### Flow Requirements vs. Prompting Reality

| Flow Requirement | Prompting Reality | Status |
|-----------------|-------------------|--------|
| Clear immediate goals | Must formulate as text BEFORE acting | ⚠ Forced pre-articulation |
| Immediate feedback | Wait for generation (seconds to minutes) | ✗ BROKEN — dead time |
| Challenge-skill balance | Challenge is linguistic, not craft-based | ⚠ Shifted domain |
| Action-awareness merger | Compose → wait → evaluate — three separate phases | ✗ BROKEN — discrete not continuous |
| Loss of self-consciousness | Constant judgment: "Is this what I wanted?" | ✗ BROKEN — evaluator never leaves |
| Sense of control | Control via language only, output unpredictable | ⚠ Indirect control |
| Time distortion | Wait cycles break temporal absorption | ✗ BROKEN — clock awareness persists |

Four of seven requirements broken. Two compromised. One shifted to a different domain.

**Prompting structurally prevents flow.**

This is not a flaw in any specific tool. It is a property of the interaction pattern: symbolic composition → delegation → wait → evaluation. Any tool with this pattern will produce the same consciousness restriction.

### The State It Imposes

Prompting locks the operator into BLACK MIRROR:
- Analytical, evaluative, judgmental
- Symbolic/linguistic processing dominant
- Self-conscious (the evaluator is always present)
- Discrete feedback cycles (not continuous)
- BPM 10-15, narrow attention, static body

BLACK MIRROR is a valid state. BLACK MIRROR is where analysis happens, where patterns get examined, where correction occurs. It is not the enemy.

But BLACK MIRROR is not BLUE RIVER. And BLUE RIVER is where:
- The hand knows what the mind doesn't
- Happy accidents happen
- Embodied knowledge expresses itself
- The unconscious contributes to the work
- The artist discovers what they're making by making it

In electromagnetic terms: prompting converts the sky into a dark room. The ambient scattering of Blue River — high energy distributed everywhere, already present before you look — is compressed into the total absorption of Black Mirror. Everything goes in. Nothing bounces back. Self-reflection replaces ambient flow.

### The Symbolic Bottleneck

There is a further restriction specific to prompting: **everything must pass through language.**

Traditional tools create direct feedback loops:

```
INTENTION → HAND MOVES → MATERIAL RESPONDS → EYE SEES → HAND ADJUSTS
(continuous, embodied, pre-linguistic)
```

Prompting creates mediated feedback loops:

```
INTENTION → TRANSLATED TO WORDS → SUBMITTED → SYSTEM PROCESSES → OUTPUT APPEARS → EVALUATED AGAINST INTENTION
(discrete, symbolic, linguistically dependent)
```

The artist working with brush, clay, instrument, or rig has a channel to the pre-linguistic. The body can express what the mind hasn't yet formulated. The tool accepts non-verbal input.

The artist prompting has NO channel to the pre-linguistic. Every intention must be articulated in symbolic language before the tool will act. The tool accepts ONLY verbal input.

This is not a limitation that better models will fix. It is structural to prompt-based interaction. The bottleneck IS the interface.

## V. THE HISTORICAL PATTERN

Every technology in the extraction chain also imposed a consciousness state shift on the activity it transformed. Layer 1 (product extraction) is documented elsewhere. Layer 2 (state restriction) follows:

| Era | Technology | Activity Before | Consciousness State Before | Activity After | Consciousness State After | What Was Lost |
|-----|-----------|----------------|--------------------------|----------------|--------------------------|---------------|
| 1440s | Printing press | Manuscript copying | BLUE RIVER (embodied, rhythmic, absorbed) | Typesetting | BLACK MIRROR (arranging symbols, evaluating layout) | The scribe's contemplative absorption |
| 1768 | Encyclopedia | Scholarship as living practice | BLUE RIVER–BLACK MIRROR cycle (study, write, teach) | Entry writing to spec | BLACK MIRROR (compress to format, meet word count) | The scholar's freedom to follow inquiry |
| 1826 | Photography | Painting/drawing from observation | BLUE RIVER (hand-eye-surface, continuous) | Composing and capturing | BLACK MIRROR (frame, evaluate, click, evaluate) | The embodied loop of mark, see, adjust, mark |
| 1920s | Radio/Cinema | Live performance, storytelling | BLUE RIVER (performer) + RED CONFUSION (audience discovering) | Broadcasting/filming | BLACK MIRROR (production) + WHITE WATCHING (passive reception) | The audience's active participation in meaning-making |
| 1990s | Internet publishing | Writing, editing, typesetting as craft | BLUE RIVER (embodied production chain) | Content management | BLACK MIRROR (compose, format, publish, check metrics) | The craftsperson's tactile relationship to the work |
| 2020s | AI prompting | Making (drawing, writing, coding, animating) | BLUE RIVER (absorbed, embodied, continuous feedback) | Requesting (prompting, evaluating, iterating) | BLACK MIRROR (symbolic, evaluative, discrete feedback) | Access to the unconscious in creative work |

### The Consistent Direction

Every shift moves in the same direction:

```
BLUE RIVER (embodied, continuous, pre-linguistic)
         ↓
BLACK MIRROR (symbolic, discrete, linguistically mediated)
```

In light terms: every technology converts ambient scattering into total absorption. The sky becomes a dark room. Blue → Black. The operator loses the state that fills everything without arriving and gains the state that absorbs everything without releasing.

Each technology replaced a continuous embodied feedback loop with a discrete symbolic evaluation loop. Each time, the debate focused on the PRODUCT ("Is photography art? Is AI art?") and ignored the PROCESS ("What happened to the consciousness state of the person doing the work?").

### What the Table Shows

The extraction is not just of labor. It is of **access to flow**. Each technology that rationalizes a creative activity simultaneously restricts the consciousness states available to the person performing it.

This is not a conspiracy. It is a structural property of rationalization itself. To make an activity more efficient, you formalize it. To formalize it, you route it through symbolic systems. To route it through symbolic systems, you require the operator to work in BLACK MIRROR. BLUE RIVER is not formalizable — it is, by definition, the state where symbolic monitoring dissolves.

Efficiency and flow are structurally opposed.

The history of technology is, among other things, the history of replacing flow with efficiency in one domain after another, while debating the outputs rather than the experience.

## VI. THE GOOD AI ARTIST — AN HONEST ASSESSMENT

Good AI artists exist. Their work can be intelligent, moving, original. The argument of this document is not that AI art isn't art.

The argument is: **what consciousness state does the tool permit, and what does that cost?**

Good AI artists compensate for the tool's BLACK MIRROR lock through several strategies:

| Strategy | What It Does | Consciousness State |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Deliberate randomness | Refuse to over-specify, accept surprise | Invites RED CONFUSION (openness to novelty) |
| Output as material | Treat AI output as raw material to work WITH (collage, paint over, composite) | Returns to BLUE RIVER when hands engage material |
| Rapid iteration without judgment | Generate fast, don't evaluate, let volume produce variety | Approaches BLUE RIVER through rhythm, reduces evaluator |
| Hybrid pipeline | AI generates → human hand finishes (digital painting, compositing) | BLACK MIRROR → BLUE RIVER transition via tool switch |
| Curation as practice | Develop eye for what works, trust instinct over analysis | WHITE WATCHING (witness without judgment) |

These strategies all have something in common: **they are workarounds for the tool's default, not features of it.**

The good AI artist is fighting the interface to access consciousness states the interface structurally blocks. Their skill is partly the skill of navigating around the tool's restrictions. This is real skill. Real creativity. Real art.

But the tool's default remains: BLACK MIRROR. Evaluate. Judge. Rephrase. Submit. Wait. Judge again.

And the vast majority of users do not know the workarounds. They use the tool as designed. They work in BLACK MIRROR. They lose BLUE RIVER. And they are told this is "democratizing creativity."

## VII. THE DEMOCRATIZATION CLAIM

"AI democratizes creativity" is the prestige claim of the current cycle.

What is actually democratized:

| Claimed | Actual |
|---------|--------|
| "Anyone can create" | Anyone can REQUEST creation from a system |
| "Creativity is accessible" | BLACK MIRROR creative activity is accessible; BLUE RIVER is now harder to access |
| "The tools are available to all" | The tools are owned by three companies; access is rented |
| "No training needed" | Training in symbolic articulation (prompting) replaces training in embodied craft |
| "More people can participate" | More people can participate in evaluation-based creative loops |

What is not democratized:
- Flow states in creative work
- Embodied knowledge transmission
- Apprenticeship and skill development
- Ownership of the means of creation
- Access to the unconscious in making

The "democratization" is real at Layer 1 — more people can produce outputs that look like creative work. It is false at Layer 2 — the consciousness experience of creative work is narrowed, not expanded.

For the full treatment of how this intersects with the philosophy of creative labor, see: [LOOKAWAY: Philosophy of Labour](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/lookaway-philosophy-labour-juan-sebastian-ni%C3%B1o-florez-hifkc/?trackingId=%2FDah0oAQSC2JGZuYhmyaPQ%3D%3D).

## VIII. THE VISIBILITY ASYMMETRY

Why does everyone debate Layer 1 and ignore Layer 2?

| Layer | Visibility | Fluency | Debate Status |
|-------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Layer 1: Product Extraction | HIGH — you can see the output, compare it to human work, argue about ownership | FLUENT — processable in seconds | Constant, heated, everywhere |
| Layer 2: Process Restriction | LOW — consciousness states are invisible, require measurement, unfamiliar vocabulary | DISFLUENT — requires new concepts, introspection, empirical data | Almost nonexistent |

The brain processes what's fluent. The debate goes where the brain goes. The deeper extraction continues unexamined.

This is not new. It is the same visibility asymmetry that has operated at every row of the historical table. The product is visible. The process is invisible. The debate follows visibility. The extraction follows the gap.

## IX. FALSIFIABILITY

This analysis makes testable claims:

1. **Prompting prevents flow:** Measurable. Monitor BPM, attention pattern, time distortion, and self-reported state during prompting vs. traditional creative work. Predict: prompting will show higher BPM, narrower attention, no time distortion, persistent self-consciousness.

2. **Good AI artists work around the default:** Observable. Interview AI artists about their process. Predict: those producing the best work will describe strategies that escape the BLACK MIRROR lock.

3. **The debate focuses on Layer 1 not Layer 2:** Documentable. Content analysis of AI discourse. Predict: overwhelming focus on outputs, ownership, employment — minimal discussion of operator consciousness states.

4. **The historical pattern repeats:** Already documented across multiple technologies. Each new case can be tested: does the technology shift the operator from BLUE RIVER to BLACK MIRROR? Does the debate focus on products not process?

Claims that are not falsifiable are not claims. These are testable.
